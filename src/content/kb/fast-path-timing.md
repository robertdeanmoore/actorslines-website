---
title: Fast Path — timing a whole scene from one short read
summary: Read a handful of your lines aloud once; the app estimates timers for all the rest.
date: 2026-07-14
---

Fast Path is a quicker alternative to timing every line one at a time in Teach or Fill-in-Teach.
You read a short sample of your lines aloud as a single batch, the app times the whole batch once,
works out how fast you read, and uses that pace to estimate a timer for every line that needs one
— instead of you speaking and timing each line individually.

## Using it

- In "Prepare this scene", tick "Fast path" above the usual "My lines only"/"Just a section"
  checkboxes — it isn't exclusive with them, so you can combine it with either scope, or leave
  both off to cover the whole scene.
- It's greyed out with an explanation if the scene doesn't have enough unprepared lines yet to be
  worth it — reading the sample would cost about as much time as just timing them one by one, so
  Fast Path only offers itself once there's real time to save.
- Tap "Missing lines only" or "All lines" as usual — both now take you into the Fast Path screen
  instead of the normal walk-through.

## Reading the sample

- Read every line shown aloud, one after another, as if you were reciting them in performance —
  at your normal pace. Don't worry about the meaning: some are cut off mid-thought (shown with
  "…") since a very long speech is trimmed to a couple of sentences so it can't dominate the
  sample on its own.
- Press Start when you're ready, and Stop once you've read the last line.

## Applying the result

- The results screen shows your reading pace and how many lines will get an estimated timer.
  Tick "Apply to the whole play" to use this same pace for every unprepared line in every scene,
  instead of just this one.

> **Tip:** These are estimates, not exact measurements. While practising in Practice mode, use
> the +/- buttons on any line to nudge its timing if it feels off.
