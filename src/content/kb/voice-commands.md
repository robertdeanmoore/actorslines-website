---
title: Voice commands — a hands-free reader
summary: Say "pause", "prompt", "resume" and more in Practice and Rehearse.
date: 2026-07-08
---

A reader usually feeds you a forgotten line and waits while you gather yourself. Actors Lines
can do the same: while you're saying your line it listens for a few spoken commands, so you
never have to reach for the screen mid-flow.

## While it's your turn (the countdown is running)

- Say "pause" on its own to stop and gather yourself.
- Say "prompt" (or just "line") to pause and have the start of your line read back to you in
  your character's voice.

> **Tip:** These only fire when the word is said alone and you then stop — so they won't trip
> just because a line happens to begin with "pause". If you keep reciting, nothing happens.

## While paused

- "resume" — pick up where you left off.
- "restart" — take the line again from the top.
- "continue" (or "carry on") — follow your Restart/Resume preference from Settings.
- "prompt" / "line" — hear the opening of the line again.
- "stop" — in Practice, shows your accuracy summary for what you've run so far; tap "Exit the
  Run" when you're ready to leave. In Rehearse it ends the run immediately.

## Manual mode (Practice only)

Practice's three-dot menu has an "Auto mode"/"Manual mode" toggle. Auto mode is the usual
behaviour — your line is scored and the run moves on by itself. Manual mode instead pauses
after your line and waits for you to say (or tap) what happens next.

- "cue again" — replays the line that cued you, then gives you another go at it.
- "continue" — scores your line and moves on, same as Auto mode does automatically.
- "stop" — ends the run, as above.

> **Tip:** Manual mode is only in Practice — Rehearse doesn't have it. Use it for a slower,
> deliberate pass where you want to retry picking up a cue on demand instead of racing a
> countdown.

## Setting it up

- Turn it on (and choose how many words a prompt reads) under Settings → "Voice commands in
  Practice and Rehearse".
- "After a pause, the button" sets whether Resume/continue picks up where you stopped or
  retakes the whole line — it also names the on-screen button (Resume or Restart).

> **Tip:** The app understands close mishears ("paws", "promt"), so you don't have to
> enunciate perfectly. If it isn't hearing you, the on-screen Pause/Resume buttons always work.
