---
title: Blending voices — make your own
summary: Mix two neural voices into a new one and give it to a character.
date: 2026-07-08
---

The natural voice pack ships ten voices, and only four of them are British. If none of them
fits a character, blend two voices into a brand-new one — say, 70% Emma with 30% George for a
deeper English female voice.

## Creating a blend

- Open a character (Characters screen), and in the voice picker tap "+ Blend voices…". (You'll
  need the natural voice pack downloaded first — the banner offers it.)
- Pick Voice A and Voice B — stock voices or blends you've already saved.
- Drag the slider to set the mix. After a couple of seconds' preparation the first time, the
  ▶ Preview plays each step instantly, so you can tune it by ear.
- Name it and tap "Save voice". It's selected for the character you were editing and appears in
  every voice picker from now on.

## Using and managing blends

- A blend works exactly like a stock voice: assign it to characters in any play, and it speaks
  their lines in Teach, Practice and Rehearse.
- Rename or delete blends via "Manage blends…" in the voice picker. Deleting one reverts any
  characters using it to the default voice.
- Blends travel with your plays: backups and cast exports carry the recipe (it's tiny), and the
  receiving phone rebuilds the voice locally.

> **Tip:** Blending costs nothing at playback — a blended voice is exactly as fast as a stock
> one. Some pairs mix better than others, so preview before you save; you can always discard
> with Cancel.
