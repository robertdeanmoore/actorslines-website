---
title: Teaching the app your script's format
summary: Correct one sample page and every later scan of that play understands your script's conventions.
date: 2026-07-14
---

Every publisher formats scripts differently — some print character names in CAPITALS, some in
Title Case; some end names with a full stop, others a colon; stage directions might be italic,
bracketed, or both. The import reader auto-detects most of this, but you can teach it your exact
script from a single sample page, which makes every later scan of that play more accurate.

## How it works

- On the Import screen, tap "Teach from a sample page" and give it one busy page — ideally one
  with several characters speaking, stage directions, and a scene ending or starting. Camera
  scan, image or a PDF page all work.
- The app reads the page and shows it back with a coloured box around everything it found:
  green for dialogue, amber for stage directions, purple for scene headings, teal for
  sound/light cues and red for anything it wasn't sure about.
- Tap any box that's wrong and say what it really is — its type, who speaks it, or "not part of
  the script" for page numbers and running titles. You can also merge a line into the one above
  if a speech was split in two, and leave a short note about anything odd ("directions are bold
  here").
- If everything looks right, just tap "Looks right — done" — confirming a correct page teaches
  the app too, by locking in what it detected.

## What it learns

- Your script's conventions: name case, the punctuation after names, whether italics can be
  trusted, bracketed directions, centred names, page margins, the running page header to
  ignore, and how the scanner misreads particular character names.
- What it learns is saved with the play (and travels with cast exports, so your castmates'
  imports benefit too). It is applied to every later scan of this play — single scenes and
  whole-play imports alike.
- On phones with on-device AI, your corrections are also shown to the AI refiner as examples of
  this script's quirks when it double-checks uncertain lines.

## Re-teaching or forgetting

- Switched to a different printed edition? Tap "Re-teach from a sample page" on the Import
  screen, or "Forget the learned format" to go back to pure auto-detection.

> **Tip:** Teaching is optional and takes about a minute. It helps most when a first scan came
> out messy — that's the sign your script's format differs from what the app expects.
