---
title: Sections — drilling part of a scene
summary: Mark a start and end line, then run just that stretch over and over.
date: 2026-07-08
---

When you sit down to work, you usually run a chunk of a scene — a page, an exchange, one
conversation — again and again. A section lets you mark that stretch once and replay just it,
without touching the rest of the scene.

## Marking a section

- Open the kebab menu (⋮, top right) and tap "Define section" to start.
- Tap the line where you want the section to start — it's created instantly, sized to however
  many of your own lines you've set as the default (Settings → Practice and Rehearse →
  "Default section length", 1–100, default 1). A brief message at the top confirms how many
  lines it was made with.
- The section is drawn right away with a "Start of section" rule and an "End of section" rule
  (both showing the line count), and a thin coloured bracket down the right margin. Drag the
  grip beside either marker to lengthen or shorten it — see "Moving a boundary" below.
- You can tap a search result instead of scrolling to pick the start line.

## Marking a speech

Open the kebab menu and tap "Define speech" to start. Unlike a section, a speech still asks
for two taps: the first line, then the last (either order; tapping the first again cancels),
then a name.

## Running a section

- Practice: a "Run section N" chip sits right on the section's own "Start of section N" row —
  scroll up to that row and tap it to run just that stretch.
- Rehearse: scroll so the section you want fills the screen. A "Run Section" button appears
  near the search bar, targeting whichever section takes up most of the screen.
- Either way it plays from the first line and stops at the last. In Practice, the run ends
  with a single "Exit the Run" button — use the "Go to top" chip below to run it again. In
  Rehearse it still offers "Run Section … Again". In Learn it simply jumps you to the top of
  the section.
- The ordinary "Run this scene" buttons are unchanged — they still run the whole scene.

> **Tip:** In Practice and Rehearse, the "End of section N" (and "End of [speech]") row has a
> "Go to top" chip — tap it to jump straight back to the start of that stretch for another
> pass, without scrolling by hand. If you've scrolled deep into a long section or speech, a
> floating copy of the same chip fades in at the bottom of the screen so you don't have to
> scroll all the way down to find it first.

## Several sections

- You can mark as many as you like; they're numbered top to bottom and each gets its own
  colour from a distinct palette — teal, amber, violet, and so on. They can't overlap or share
  a boundary line.
- By default, sections you mark in Practice also apply in Rehearse — Rehearse just displays
  them, and its own section controls (Define section, drag, delete) are turned off there; edit
  sections in Practice instead. Turn off "Align sections across modes" (Settings → Practice and
  Rehearse) to give Practice and Rehearse independent sections again — turning it back on later
  replaces every scene's Rehearse sections with Practice's, so it asks you to confirm first.
  Learn's sections stay separate either way.

> **Tip:** While aligned, the Scene Editor also shows your Practice sections as light grey
> "ghost" chips — start and end only, no colour, no actions — just so you can see where they
> fall while editing lines. Turn off alignment and they disappear from the editor too.

> **Tip:** In Practice and Rehearse, the scroll bar on the right edge is tinted with each
> section's colour along the stretch it covers — a quick map of where your sections are as you
> drag up and down.

## Section actions

Tapping the "Start of section" label opens a small menu:

- Show/Hide All — the same five reveal options described in "Progressive line reveal" (Show
  All, Hide All, First word, First letters, Random), applied to just this section's lines after
  a confirmation. Works the same as the scene-wide kebab action but scoped to the section.
- Delete section — leads to a confirmation; your lines are untouched.

> **Tip:** Both actions are unavailable on a plain section in Rehearse while sections are
> aligned with Practice — tapping delete there just explains where to go instead.

## Moving a boundary

Picked the wrong line to start or end on? In Practice and Rehearse, every start and end
(sections and speeches alike) has a small grip icon beside it. Long-press the grip and drag up
or down to nudge that boundary to a different line; drag near the top or bottom of the screen
and the list scrolls itself to follow you.

> **Tip:** A plain section's grip doesn't appear in Rehearse while sections are aligned with
> Practice — drag it there instead. Speech grips are unaffected either way.

The boundary can't be dragged past the top or bottom of the scene, past another section of the
same kind, or past its own other end — it simply stops there, so you never have to undo an
accidental overlap. Lift your finger to drop it in place.

> **Tip:** Sections stay put when you leave the screen and travel with both a personal backup
> and an "Export for cast" file, so the way you've carved up a scene is always there when you
> come back — and your cast mates get the same sections when you share the play.

> **Tip:** A section's start and end labels both show a live count of your own lines in the
> stretch — "Lines: n" — updating as you drag a boundary, so you can see exactly how much
> you're including before you let go. A speech's opening label shows "Words: n" instead, so you
> can gauge how much there is to learn before you dive in.

> **Tip:** Tapping a speech in the scenes list's Speeches panel opens it with its first line at
> the very top of the screen, rather than centred like a normal bookmark — a speech is
> self-contained, so you don't need to see what comes before it.
