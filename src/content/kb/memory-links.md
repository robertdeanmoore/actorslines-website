It's common to know your own line but blank on the cue that should trigger it. Memory links
asks your phone's on-device AI to suggest a short, memorable association between the line
spoken just before yours and your own line — a word they share, a cause-and-effect link, a
pun, or a vivid image combining both.

## Getting a suggestion

- In Practice, long-press the left margin next to one of your own lines. Nothing is shown
  there until you've saved a hint — it's a blank, invisible touch target until then.
- The dialog shows the cue lines it gathered automatically. Tap the ✕ next to one to drop it,
  or "Add previous line" to pull in one more, before tapping "Get suggestions".
- Flick through the emoji + short-phrase candidates and tap the one that clicks for you. "More
  ideas" asks for a fresh batch instead. None of them auto-apply — you choose.

## Managing a saved hint

- Once saved, the emoji shows in that same left margin every time you practice the line.
  Long-press it again for "Change" (ask again) or "Remove" (delete it).

> **Tip:** Only available on phones with on-device AI (Gemini Nano); turn it off entirely
> under Settings → "Suggest memory links with on-device AI" if you'd rather not see it.
