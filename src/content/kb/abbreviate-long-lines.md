---
title: Abbreviate long other-character lines
summary: Shorten another character's long speeches during TTS playback instead of listening in full every time.
date: 2026-07-08
---

Some scenes have another character speak at length before your cue. Once you know the gist,
listening to the whole speech on every practice pass wastes time. Turn this on in Settings and
set a word limit — any other character's line longer than that limit is shortened during TTS
playback in Practice and Rehearse.

## How the shortened line sounds

- The opening third of your word limit is spoken from the start of the line.
- A quick double tap plays — the same tap sound used for a skipped line.
- The closing two-thirds of your word limit is spoken from the end of the line — the words
  that lead straight into your cue.
- Everything in between is never spoken at all, not just paused over, so you actually save
  time rather than just waiting through a silence.

## What you see

- A qualifying line always shows the same split in the line list: the opening words, an
  italic grey "Skip - abbreviated" cue, then the closing words — all inline in the line itself,
  not a separate card like a Skip marker.
- Tap the "Skip - abbreviated" cue to see the line's full, unabbreviated text plus a reminder
  of why it's shortened. Unlike a "Skip - line hide" card, there's no Unskip option — turn the
  setting off (or raise the word limit) in Settings instead.

> **Tip:** A line you've marked "Skip this line" is never abbreviated — the skip always wins,
> even if "Include skipped lines" means it's otherwise played in full.

> **Tip:** This only affects the synthesised voice. If Selftape is set to play your own
> recordings in runs, a recorded cue always plays in full — a single audio file can't be split
> like this.

> **Tip:** The inline notation shows as soon as a line qualifies, even while just browsing the
> scene before pressing Run — it's independent of the show/hide study aid on your own lines,
> which only ever affects your own lines, never another character's.
