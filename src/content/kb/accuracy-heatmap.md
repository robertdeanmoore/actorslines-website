---
title: Accuracy overview — the practice heatmap
summary: See which scenes you've drilled a lot, and which ones you're actually getting right.
date: 2026-07-08
---

The bar-chart icon on a play's card in the Play List opens "Accuracy Overview" — a map of
every scene in the play showing how much you've practised it and how well you're doing, built
from your Practice and Rehearse run history. The whole map fits on screen at once — no
scrolling, however long the play is.

## Reading the map

- Scenes run top to bottom in order, each one's stretch of the map sized to how many of your
  own lines it holds. Every one of your lines gets its own equal-height sliver, so a long scene
  isn't squashed into the same handful of bands as a short one — sliver thickness is the same
  everywhere in the chart.
- A thin line runs the full width of the screen — across both bars and the scene-label gap
  between them — wherever one scene ends and the next begins.
- The left column, "How much practised", shades from red (untouched) to blue (your
  most-practised line) by session count.
- The right column, "How well done", uses the same green/amber/red you see elsewhere (set in
  Settings → Accuracy rating colours) for average accuracy.
- A small legend at the bottom of the screen explains what each colour means.

## Filtering and drilling in

- The Practice / Rehearsal / Both chips at the top scope the map to one run mode or both.
- Tap anywhere in a scene's stretch to see that scene's session count and average accuracy,
  plus "Practice this scene", "Rehearse this scene" and "Edit this scene" buttons that jump
  straight to it — Practice and Rehearse pick up where you last left off in that scene, the
  same way they always do; Edit opens at the top.

## Clearing and viewing by time window

- The ⋮ menu at the top has two sections, both scoped to the play you're currently looking at.
  "Clear data" deletes accuracy scores — "All time" wipes this play's whole history,
  "Last 24 hours"/"Last 7 days"/"Last 4 weeks" delete just that recent window, and "Older
  than…" lets you type a day count to delete everything further back than that — handy for
  clearing out early test runs while keeping recent practice. Every option asks you to confirm
  first and can't be undone.
- "View data" offers the same five windows, but as a display filter instead of a delete — both
  columns recompute to show only accuracy data from that window. Your choice sticks until you
  change it again, even after leaving the screen.
