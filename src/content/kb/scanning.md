---
title: Scanning & importing scripts
summary: Page layouts, image types, and how to get a clean import.
date: 2026-07-08
---

Actors Lines reads your script on the device — nothing is uploaded — and keeps the layout so
it can tell apart character cues, dialogue and stage directions.

## What you can import

- A PDF of the script (works best, especially for a whole play).
- Photos or image files of the pages (JPEG or PNG).
- A fresh scan taken with your phone camera using the built-in scanner.

## Page layouts it understands

- One script page per scan — an ordinary single page.
- Two pages per scan — an A5 booklet opened flat, giving two side-by-side pages on one A4
  sheet. Tick "Two pages per sheet (A5 book)" on the import screen and the app turns each sheet
  the right way up, trims the dark border, straightens it and splits it into a left page then a
  right page (lower page number on the left).

> **Tip:** The toggle is the reliable way to import a booklet — leave it on for the whole book.
> In the whole-play wizard, if a sheet still looks unsure it shows a quick preview so you can
> rotate it and drag the dividing line onto the centre gap before the text is read.

## Getting the best results

- Aim for about 300 DPI when scanning. Much lower and small print gets unreliable.
- Scan in colour if you've highlighted your own lines — colour lets the app pick up your
  highlighter. If the page is plain black text, monochrome/grey is perfectly fine.
- Keep book pages flat. Curved pages near the spine bend the text and the reader struggles
  there — press the book down, or photograph one page at a time held flat.
- Good even light, no heavy shadow, and as straight as you can manage. Small tilts and a black
  border from an open lid are fine — the app corrects them — but straighter is better.
- Keep all pages the same way up within one import.

## Add your characters first

Set up the play's characters before you import. Import matches each line to a character you've
already created — it never invents them.

- Add your cast on the play's Characters list (name, who plays it, which one is you).
- If a play has no characters yet, the import screen asks you to add them first and gives you
  an "Add characters" button.
- Any speaker the reader can't match to one of your characters is kept and flagged as unknown
  in the review step, where you tap it onto the right character.

## What happens after it reads the pages

- Italic text is treated as stage directions; sound and light cues are spotted too.
- Each line's speaker is matched to your existing characters and given their ink colour.
- Importing one scene drops you straight into a review list. Importing a whole play walks you
  through marking the start, the end and each scene boundary first.
- Scene boundaries are usually spotted automatically from "ACT"/"SCENE" headings. On phones with
  on-device AI, a badly scanned heading it would otherwise miss gets a second look — you still
  confirm every boundary before it's used.
- You always review before anything is saved: tap a line to edit or re-label it, reorder, skip,
  merge or split, or reassign an unknown speaker — so mistakes are quick to tidy.

> **Tip:** In short: a flat, well-lit, ~300 DPI page (colour if highlighted) gives the cleanest
> import — and a quick review at the end catches anything the reader got wrong.
